1. Massively parallel assays require constructing large DNA sequence libraries.
    - MPRAs often involve large libraries of synthetic promoters that can be constructed in a wide variety of ways.
        - Partial random mutagenesis of an endogenous regulatory element
        - Scanning deletions, insertions, or scrambling of regulatory sequences
        - Random ligation of TF binding sites
        - Random barcodes for experimental readout
    - Deep mutational scanning (DMS) experiments often involve similar but distinct complex libraries.
        - Saturation mutagenesis involving all single- or double- mutations of amino acids or codons.
        - Recoding targeted DNA sequences.
    - These libraries are often constructed using designed oligonucleotide pool.
        - Multiple companies will now synthesize computationally designed mixed pools of oligonucleotides. 
        - Except for very standard types of libraries, however, it is up to the user to design the individual sequences and provide those sequences as a .csv file. 
1. Interpreting genomic AI models increasingly relies on in silico DNA sequence libraries.
    - To study a genomic AI model, one can computationally construct variant DNA sequences, feed those sequences to the model, and study model response as a function of sequence content. 
    - Peter Koo's group in particular has established the utility of this type of study [Describe CREME]. 
    - Another approach is called surrogate modeling, where simpler interpretable models are used to approximate the behavior of genomic AI models over a restricted set of sequences. [Describe SEAM]
1. Computationally designing oligonucleotide pools is frankly kind of annoying.
    - Typically one writes a Python or R script that starts with a wild-type sequence of interest and transforms it in different ways.
    - The design of simple libraries isn't hard, but it's tedious, error-prone, and requires someone with programming skill.
    - One generally has to write individual little functions or scripts that perform each of these sequence transformations, then collates the results.
    - In addition, one often wants to name each of the variant sequences for later reference and computational analysis. 
    - People have developed software to help generate specific types of sequence variants, but the general problem of how to efficiently construct DNA sequence libraries remains unaddressed. 
1. Deep learning frameworks suggested a way of tacklking the DNA design problem.
    - In these DL frameworks, the core objects are tensors, which conceptually represent multi-dimensional arrays of numbers. 
    - One constructs a DL model by starting with one or more input tensor, then writing code that transforms those tensors into an output tensor.
    - Under the hood each ouput tensor represents more than just the array of numbers -- it represents a computational graph describing all the steps needed to calcualte its contents from input contents. 
    - By representing the entire graph, the DL frameworks can carry out operations like back-propagation that are needed for model training.
